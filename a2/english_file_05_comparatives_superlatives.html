CEFR: A2-B1

Lesson 5: Comparatives & Superlatives

📚 1. Grammar Explanation（文法の説明）

1. Comparatives（比較級）

We use comparatives to show differences between two things. → 2つのものを比べるときに使います。

FormExample
short adj + -erfaster, taller
more + long adjmore interesting
less + adjectiveless crowded
(not) as + adj + asnot as big as

2. Superlatives（最上級）

We use superlatives to show the highest level in a group. → グループの中で一番〜を表す。

FormExample
the + adj + -estthe tallest
the most + adjthe most beautiful
the least + adjthe least expensive

3. Quantifiers（数量表現）

We use quantifiers to talk about amount. → 量について話すときに使います。

  • too much + uncountable noun → too much water
  • too many + countable noun → too many cars
  • too + adjective → too cold
  • enough before nouns / after adjectives → enough time / big enough

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences（短い例文）

  • This bag is heavier than mine.
    • このかばんは私のより重い。
  • My sister is more patient than me.
    • 姉は私よりがまん強い。
  • Winter is the coldest season here.
    • ここでは冬が一番寒い。
  • This café is the most popular in town.
    • このカフェは町で一番人気です。
  • I drank too much coffee today.
    • 今日はコーヒーを飲みすぎた。
  • There are too many people on the train.
    • 電車に人が多すぎる。
  • The room isn’t big enough for five people.
    • その部屋は5人には十分大きくない。

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences（長い例文）

  • My new phone is much faster than my old one, so I can work more easily.
    • 新しいスマホは前のよりずっと速く、作業がしやすいです。
  • This restaurant is the most comfortable place to study in the neighborhood.
    • このレストランは近所で一番勉強しやすい場所です。
  • I bought too many snacks for the trip, so my bag became very heavy.
    • 旅行のためにお菓子を買いすぎて、かばんがとても重くなりました。
  • Our classroom isn’t warm enough in winter, so students often wear jackets.
    • 冬は教室が十分暖かくなく、生徒はよく上着を着ています。
  • My brother works harder this year than he did last year because he wants a promotion.
    • 昇進したいので、兄は去年より今年のほうが一生懸命働いています。

🧩 4. Practice Exercises（練習問題）

Exercise A – Choose the correct option

1. This test is ________ than the last one. (easier / easy)
easier
2. My sister is ________ in our family. (the tallest / taller)
the tallest
3. There are ________ cars on the road today. (too many / too much)
too many

enough

📖 5. Short Reading Text（短い読解文）

A Crowded Day at the New Mall

Last weekend, my friends and I visited a new shopping mall in our city. It was much bigger than the old one, and the stores were more modern. However, there were too many people, so it was difficult to walk around. We found a café on the top floor, and it was the most relaxing place in the whole building. I ordered a sandwich, but it was too expensive, so I didn’t buy anything else. After that, we went to a bookstore. The travel section was the most interesting part for me because I love reading about other countries. Even though the mall was crowded, we had a good time, and I think I’ll go again on a quieter day.

新しいショッピングモールでの一日

先週末、友達と新しいショッピングモールに行きました。前のモールよりずっと大きく、お店ももっと現代的でした。しかし、人が多すぎて歩くのが大変でした。最上階にカフェがあり、建物の中で一番リラックスできる場所でした。サンドイッチを注文しましたが、高すぎたので他には何も買いませんでした。その後、本屋に行きました。旅行コーナーは一番おもしろく、私は外国について読むのが好きです。混んでいましたが楽しい時間を過ごせたので、もっと静かな日にまた行こうと思います。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions（読解問題）

1. Why was it difficult to walk in the mall?

なぜ歩きにくかったですか？

Because there were too many people.
2. Where was the most relaxing place?

一番リラックスできる場所はどこでしたか？

The café on the top floor.
3. Why didn’t the writer buy more food?

なぜもっと食べ物を買いませんでしたか？

Because it was too expensive.
4. What was the most interesting part of the bookstore?

本屋で一番おもしろかった場所は？

The travel section.
5. Does the writer want to visit the mall again?

また行きたいと思っていますか？

Yes, on a quieter day.

🎯 7. Summary（まとめ）

  • Comparatives compare two things.
    • 2つを比べる。
  • Superlatives show the highest level.
    • グループで一番。
  • Quantifiers describe amount.
    • 量を表す。
  • too / too much / too many = 過剰。
    • 多すぎることを表す。
  • enough = 十分。
    • 必要な量がある。
