CEFR: A2-B1

S1: Conditionals – Zero, First, Second & Third

📚 1. Grammar Explanation

1) Zero Conditional – Facts and general truths

We use the Zero Conditional to talk about things that are always true, facts, or rules. Both clauses use the present simple.

ゼロ条件は「いつもそうなること・事実・ルール」に使います。両方とも現在形です。

Form Example
If + present simple, present simple If you heat ice, it melts.

2) First Conditional – Real future possibilities

We use the First Conditional to talk about real and possible situations in the future. The if-clause is present simple, and the result clause uses will.

ファースト条件は「現実的な未来の可能性」に使います。if節は現在形、結果は will を使います。

Form Example
If + present simple, will + base verb If it rains, we will stay at home.

3) Second Conditional – Unreal or unlikely present/future

We use the Second Conditional to talk about unreal or very unlikely situations now or in the future. The if-clause uses the past simple, and the result clause uses would.

セカンド条件は「今・未来の非現実的／可能性が低い仮定」に使います。if節は過去形、結果は would を使います。

Form Example
If + past simple, would + base verb If I had more time, I would learn another language.

4) Third Conditional – Unreal past situations

We use the Third Conditional to talk about unreal situations in the past and their imaginary results. The if-clause uses had + past participle, and the result clause uses would have + past participle.

サード条件は「過去に実際は起こらなかったこと」を想像して話すときに使います。

Form Example
If + had + past participle, would have + past participle If she had left earlier, she would have caught the train.

※A2レベルでは、サード条件は「形と意味を知る」レベルでOKです。

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences

• If you mix blue and yellow, you get green.

青と黄色を混ぜると、緑になります。（ゼロ条件）

• If I finish my homework, I will watch a movie.

宿題を終えたら、映画を見ます。（ファースト条件）

• If it is sunny tomorrow, we will go to the park.

明日晴れたら、公園に行きます。（ファースト条件）

• If I were rich, I would buy a small island.

もしお金持ちなら、小さな島を買うのに。（セカンド条件）

• If she had more free time, she would learn piano.

もっと自由な時間があれば、ピアノを習うのに。（セカンド条件）

• If he had studied harder, he would have passed the exam.

もっと勉強していれば、試験に合格していたのに。（サード条件）

• If they had left earlier, they would have avoided the traffic.

もっと早く出ていれば、渋滞を避けられたのに。（サード条件）

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences

• If you don’t water the plants for many days, they slowly die and the leaves turn brown.

何日も水をあげないと、植物は少しずつ枯れて葉が茶色になります。（ゼロ条件）

• If I pass the English test this semester, I will celebrate with my friends at a small restaurant.

今学期の英語テストに合格したら、小さなレストランで友だちとお祝いします。（ファースト条件）

• If my parents visit Tokyo next year, I will show them my favorite places and cafés.

両親が来年東京に来たら、私のお気に入りの場所やカフェを案内します。（ファースト条件）

• If I lived near the ocean, I would go for a walk on the beach every evening after work.

海の近くに住んでいたら、仕事のあと毎晩ビーチを散歩するのに。（セカンド条件）

• If we had booked the tickets earlier, we would have found cheaper seats on the same flight.

もっと早くチケットを予約していれば、同じ便でもっと安い席が見つかったのに。（サード条件）

🧩 4. Practice Exercises

Exercise A – Choose the correct option

正しい形を選びましょう。（かっこ内から選ぶ）

1. If you (heat / heated) ice, it melts.

heat

2. If it (rains / rained) tomorrow, we will stay at home.

rains

3. If I (have / had) more money, I would buy a new laptop.

had

4. If she (studies / studied) harder, she will pass the exam.

studies

5. If they had left earlier, they (would catch / would have caught) the train.

would have caught

Exercise B – Complete the sentence

文を完成させましょう。動詞の形に注意してください。

1. If you ________ (touch) a hot pan, you get burned.

touch

2. If I finish work early, I ________ (meet) my friend for dinner.

will meet

3. If I were taller, I ________ (play) basketball on the school team.

would play

4. If he had listened to the teacher, he ________ (understand) the homework.

would have understood

5. If you don’t bring an umbrella, you ________ (get) wet if it rains.

will get

📖 5. Short Reading Text

Emma’s Weekend Plans and Possibilities

Emma loves planning her weekends, but the weather often changes her plans. If it is sunny, she goes to the park with her friends and they play badminton. If it rains, she stays at home and reads a book or watches a movie. She sometimes thinks about other possibilities too. If she lived near the sea, she would walk on the beach every Saturday morning. If she had more money, she would travel to different countries during long holidays. Last year, she wanted to visit her grandparents, but she didn’t check the train schedule. If she had checked it earlier, she would have arrived on time for dinner. Now she always says, “If I plan well, I will enjoy my weekend more.”

エマの週末と「もし〜なら」

エマは週末の計画を立てるのが大好きですが、天気によって予定がよく変わります。晴れていれば友だちと公園に行き、バドミントンをします。雨なら家にいて本を読んだり映画を見たりします。彼女はほかの可能性について考えることもあります。もし海の近くに住んでいれば、毎週土曜日の朝にビーチを散歩するでしょう。もっとお金があれば、長い休みにいろいろな国へ旅行するでしょう。去年、祖父母の家を訪ねたかったのですが、電車の時刻表を確認しませんでした。もし早く時刻表を確認していれば、夕食に間に合っていたのにと思っています。今ではいつも「ちゃんと計画すれば、週末をもっと楽しめる」と言っています。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions

英文を読んで、質問に答えましょう。ボタンを押すと答えが表示されます。

1. What does Emma do if it is sunny?

晴れているとき、エマは何をしますか。

She goes to the park with her friends and they play badminton.

2. What does she do if it rains?

雨のとき、エマは何をしますか。

She stays at home and reads a book or watches a movie.

3. What would Emma do every Saturday morning if she lived near the sea?

もし海の近くに住んでいれば、毎週土曜日の朝に何をするでしょうか。

She would walk on the beach every Saturday morning.

4. What would she do during long holidays if she had more money?

もっとお金があれば、長い休みに何をするでしょうか。

She would travel to different countries during long holidays.

5. What would have happened if she had checked the train schedule earlier?

もし早く電車の時刻表を確認していれば、どうなっていたでしょうか。

She would have arrived on time for dinner with her grandparents.

🎯 Summary（まとめ）

  • Zero Conditional talks about facts and things that are always true.
    ゼロ条件は、いつもそうなる事実やルールについて話すときに使います。
  • First Conditional talks about real and possible situations in the future.
    ファースト条件は、未来の現実的な可能性について話すときに使います。
  • Second Conditional talks about unreal or unlikely situations now or in the future.
    セカンド条件は、今・未来の非現実的／可能性の低い状況について話すときに使います。
  • Third Conditional talks about unreal situations in the past and their imaginary results.
    サード条件は、過去に実際は起こらなかったことと、その想像上の結果について話すときに使います。
  • Pay attention to the verb forms after if and in the result clause.
    if のあとの動詞の形と、結果の文の動詞の形に注意しましょう。
