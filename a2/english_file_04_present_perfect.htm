CEFR: A2-B1

Lesson 4: Present Perfect

📚 1. Grammar Explanation（文法の説明）

1. Present Perfect（現在完了形）

We use the Present Perfect to talk about actions that are finished but connected to now. → 「今につながる過去の行動」を表す。

FormExample
have / has + past participleI have finished my homework.
ContractionsI've, You've, He's, She's, We've, They've

2. yet / just / already（時間のニュアンス）

  • yet → 疑問文・否定文、文末
  • just → 「たった今」、動詞の前
  • already → 「もうすでに」、動詞の前

3. Present Perfect vs Past Simple（現在完了 vs 過去形）

  • 現在完了 → 経験（時間を言わない）
  • 過去形 → いつを言う
  • been = 行って戻った / gone = 行って今そこにいる

4. something / anything / nothing

  • some- → 肯定文
  • any- → 疑問文・否定文
  • no- → "not any" の意味

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences（短い例文）

  • I've just finished my homework.
    • たった今宿題を終えました。
  • Have you eaten yet?
    • もう食べましたか？
  • She's already left the office.
    • 彼女はもうオフィスを出ました。
  • We've been to Kyoto three times.
    • 私たちは京都に3回行ったことがあります。
  • He hasn't called yet.
    • 彼はまだ電話していません。
  • There's something in my bag.
    • かばんの中に何かあります。
  • I didn't go anywhere yesterday.
    • 昨日はどこにも行きませんでした。

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences（長い例文）

  • I've just cleaned my room, so it looks very tidy now.
    • たった今部屋を掃除したので、とてもきれいです。
  • Have you ever stayed in a traditional Japanese inn?
    • 旅館に泊まったことがありますか？
  • She's already finished the report, but she hasn't sent it yet.
    • 彼女はもうレポートを終えましたが、まだ送っていません。
  • We didn't buy anything because we had already spent too much.
    • お金を使いすぎていたので、何も買いませんでした。
  • There was nobody in the classroom, so I studied somewhere quiet.
    • 教室には誰もいなかったので、静かな場所で勉強しました。

🧩 4. Practice Exercises（練習問題）

Exercise A – Choose the correct option

1. I've ______ my lunch already. (eat / eaten)
eaten
2. Have you ______ to Osaka before? (been / gone)
been
3. There's ______ in my shoe. (something / anything)
something
4. We didn't go ______ last weekend. (somewhere / anywhere)
anywhere
5. ______ called while you were out. (Somebody / Nobody)
Somebody

Exercise B – Complete the sentence

1. I ____________________________ yet. (not finish / my report)
I haven't finished my report yet.
2. She ____________________________ a new smartphone. (just / buy)
She has just bought a new smartphone.
3. We ____________________________ that movie three times. (already / see)
We have already seen that movie three times.
4. ____________________________ to Hokkaido? (you / ever / be)
Have you ever been to Hokkaido?
5. There is ____________________________ in the fridge. (nothing / interesting)
There is nothing interesting in the fridge.

📖 5. Short Reading Text（短い読解文）

A Quiet Evening at Home

I've just come home from work, and I'm really tired. I've already changed into comfortable clothes, and I've made a cup of tea. My phone has rung twice, but I haven't answered it yet because I want a quiet evening. There is nothing interesting on TV, so I've decided to read a book instead. I've never read this author before, but a friend recommended the book to me last week. Now I'm sitting by the window, enjoying the warm tea. Today has been busy, but I finally feel relaxed.

静かな夜のひととき

仕事から帰ってきて、とても疲れています。もう楽な服に着替えて、お茶を入れました。電話が二回鳴りましたが、静かな夜にしたいのでまだ出ていません。テレビにはおもしろい番組がないので、本を読むことにしました。この作家の本は初めてですが、友だちが先週すすめてくれました。今は窓のそばでお茶を楽しんでいます。忙しい一日でしたが、ようやく落ち着きました。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions（読解問題）

1. Why hasn't the writer answered the phone yet?
なぜまだ電話に出ていないのですか？
Because they want a quiet evening.
2. What has the writer already done?
書き手はすでに何をしましたか？
They have changed clothes and made tea.
3. Why did the writer choose to read a book?
なぜ本を読むことにしたのですか？
Because there was nothing interesting on TV.
4. Has the writer read this author before?
この作家の本を読んだことがありますか？
No, they haven't.
5. How does the writer feel at the end?
最後に書き手はどう感じていますか？
Relaxed.

🎯 7. Summary（まとめ）

  • Present Perfect connects past actions with now.
    • 現在完了は「今につながる過去」。
  • Use yet / just / already for time nuance.
    • 時間のニュアンスを表す語。
  • Present Perfect = experience / Past Simple = specific time.
    • 現在完了＝経験、過去形＝いつを言う。
  • something / anything / nothing express unknown people/things/places.
    • 不明な人・物・場所を表す。
