CEFR: B1-B2

Lesson 8: Relative Clauses

📚 1. Grammar Explanation（文法の説明）

1. Defining relative clauses（限定用法）

We use relative clauses to give essential information about a noun. They tell us exactly which person or thing we mean. → 名詞を特定するための「必要な情報」を加えるときに使います。

PatternExample
who = peopleThe woman who lives next door is a doctor.
which = thingsThe book which I bought yesterday is very interesting.
that = people or thingsThe company that hired me is in Tokyo.
where = placesThe café where we met is closed now.

Commas are NOT used in defining relative clauses. → 限定用法ではカンマを使いません。

2. Non-defining relative clauses（非限定用法）

Non-defining clauses add extra, non-essential information. If we remove the clause, the sentence is still complete. → なくても意味は通じる「追加情報」をカンマで挟んで説明します。

PatternExample
whoMy brother, who lives in Osaka, is visiting next week.
whichMy laptop, which I bought last year, is already slow.
whereKyoto, where many tourists go, is beautiful in autumn.

We always use commas with non-defining clauses. → 非限定用法では必ずカンマで区切ります。「, who … ,」のように。

3. Natural style: who / which / that / where（自然な使い方）

In spoken English, that is very common in defining clauses, especially after common nouns. → 会話では限定用法で that をよく使います。 We do NOT use that in non-defining clauses. → 非限定用法（カンマ付き）では that は使いません。 We often omit the relative pronoun when it is the object. → 目的語のときは省略できることも多いです（例：The book (which) I read）。

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences（短い例文）

  • The teacher who explained the grammar was very clear.
    • 文法を説明してくれた先生はとても分かりやすかったです。
  • The movie that we watched yesterday was too long.
    • 昨日見た映画は長すぎました。
  • This is the café where I usually study.
    • ここは私がいつも勉強しているカフェです。
  • My sister, who lives abroad, is coming home for New Year.
    • 海外に住んでいる妹が正月に帰ってきます。
  • That book, which you recommended, was really helpful.
    • あなたが勧めてくれたその本はとても役に立ちました。
  • The company that hired me is expanding quickly.
    • 私を採用した会社は急速に成長しています。
  • Kyoto, where many temples are located, is popular with tourists.
    • 多くの寺がある京都は観光客に人気です。

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences（長い例文）

  • The colleague who sits next to me always helps me with difficult tasks, which makes my work much easier.
    • 隣に座っている同僚は、難しい仕事をいつも手伝ってくれるので、仕事がとても楽になります。
  • The restaurant that we went to last night serves traditional dishes, which are popular with both locals and tourists.
    • 昨日行ったレストランは伝統的な料理を出していて、地元の人にも観光客にも人気があります。
  • My smartphone, which I bought only six months ago, already has some battery problems.
    • たった6か月前に買ったスマートフォンなのに、すでにバッテリーの問題があります。
  • The city where I grew up has changed a lot, which sometimes makes me feel nostalgic and a little sad.
    • 私が育った街は大きく変わっていて、ときどき懐かしく、少し寂しい気持ちになります。
  • Our manager, who has worked in several countries, often shares stories that inspire the whole team.
    • いくつかの国で働いた経験があるマネージャーは、チーム全体を励ますような話をよくしてくれます。

🧩 4. Practice Exercises（練習問題）

Exercise A – Choose the correct option

1. The woman ______ lives upstairs is a designer. (who / which / where)
who
2. This is the park ______ we first met. (who / which / where)
where
3. The laptop ______ I bought last year is already slow. (who / which / that)
which / that
4. My brother, ______ lives in Canada, is visiting next month. (who / that / where)
who
5. Kyoto, ______ many old temples are located, is beautiful in autumn. (who / which / where)
where

Exercise B – Complete the sentence

1. The book ______ ______ (I / read) was written by a Japanese author.
The book which/that I read was written by a Japanese author.
2. This is the café ______ ______ (we / meet / last time).
This is the café where we met last time.
3. My friend, ______ ______ (lives / in Osaka), is a web designer.
My friend, who lives in Osaka, is a web designer.
4. The company ______ ______ (hired / me) is very international.
The company that/which hired me is very international.
5. Tokyo, ______ ______ (I / was born), is always changing.
Tokyo, where I was born, is always changing.

📖 5. Short Reading Text（短い読解文）

Living in a Modern City

The city where I live now is very different from the town where I grew up. I live in an area that is full of modern buildings and busy streets, which can be exciting but also tiring. My office, which is located near the station, is in a building that has many international companies. I often meet people who come from different countries and cultures, which makes my daily life more interesting.

There is a small park that I visit during my lunch break, where I can relax and clear my mind. The park, which has a few old trees and a quiet pond, reminds me of the countryside where my grandparents live. Although I sometimes miss the place where I spent my childhood, I also enjoy the opportunities that this city offers, which help me grow both personally and professionally.

現代的な都市での生活

今住んでいる街は、私が育った町とはとても違います。 私が住んでいるエリアは近代的な建物と忙しい通りでいっぱいで、刺激的な一方で少し疲れることもあります。 駅の近くにあるオフィスビルには多くの国際的な会社が入っていて、私はさまざまな国や文化から来た人たちとよく会います。 それが日常生活をよりおもしろくしてくれます。

昼休みによく行く小さな公園があり、そこでリラックスして頭をリセットします。 その公園には古い木と静かな池があり、祖父母が住んでいる田舎を思い出させてくれます。 子ども時代を過ごした場所が恋しくなることもありますが、この街が与えてくれる多くのチャンスのおかげで、私は個人的にも仕事面でも成長できていると感じています。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions（読解問題）

1) How is the city where the writer lives now different from their hometown?
It is full of modern buildings and busy streets, unlike the town where they grew up.
2) Where is the writer’s office located?
It is located near the station in a building with many international companies.
3) Why does the writer enjoy meeting people at work?
Because they come from different countries and cultures, which makes daily life more interesting.
4) What reminds the writer of the countryside where their grandparents live?
A small park with old trees and a quiet pond.
5) Why does the writer feel positive about living in the city?
Because the city offers many opportunities that help them grow personally and professionally.

🎯 7. Summary（まとめ）

  • Defining clauses give essential information and do not use commas.
    • 限定用法は名詞を特定し、カンマを使いません。
  • Non-defining clauses add extra information and are separated by commas.
    • 非限定用法は追加情報で、カンマで区切ります。
  • Who / which / that / where are used for people, things, and places in natural English.
    • who／which／that／where を人・物・場所に自然に使い分けます。
  • We do not use that in non-defining clauses.
    • 非限定用法では that は使いません。
