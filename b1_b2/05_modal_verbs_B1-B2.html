CEFR: B1-B2

Lesson 5: Modal Verbs

📚 1. Grammar Explanation（文法の説明）

1. Must / Have to（義務・必要性）

Must expresses a strong personal obligation or something the speaker feels is essential. → 話し手自身が「強く必要だ」と感じる義務。 Have to refers to external rules, requirements, or situations that force the obligation. → ルール・状況によって生じる義務。

UseExample
Must (internal obligation)I must finish this report today; it’s very important to me.
Have to (external rule)Employees have to follow safety regulations at all times.

2. Should（助言・期待）

Should is used for giving advice, making recommendations, or expressing what is expected or appropriate. → 助言・おすすめ・「〜すべきだ」という期待。

UseExample
Advice / recommendationYou should take breaks more often to avoid stress.
ExpectationThe train should arrive on time, but it’s sometimes delayed.

3. May / Might / Could（可能性の度合い）

May, Might, Could all express possibility, but with different levels of certainty. → 可能性の強さが異なる。 May = fairly likely (50–60%) Might = weaker possibility (30–40%) Could = theoretically possible, but uncertain → ニュアンスの違いを理解することが大切。

UseExample
Moderate possibilityIt may rain later, so take an umbrella just in case.
Weaker possibilityHe might join us if he finishes work early.
General possibilityThe rumor could be true, but we need more information.

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences（短い例文）

  • You must finish your homework before you can join the meeting.
    • 会議に参加する前に宿題を終えなければなりません。
  • We have to leave at 8 because the train doesn’t wait.
    • 電車は待ってくれないので、8時に出発しなければなりません。
  • You should sleep earlier if you want to feel more energetic tomorrow.
    • 明日元気に過ごしたいなら、もっと早く寝たほうがいいです。
  • It may snow tonight, so driving could be dangerous.
    • 今夜雪が降るかもしれないので、運転が危険になる可能性があります。
  • He might be busy because he hasn’t replied all day.
    • 一日中返信がないので、彼は忙しいのかもしれません。
  • She could be at the library preparing for her exam.
    • 彼女は試験勉強のために図書館にいる可能性があります。
  • You must not touch that equipment; it’s very sensitive.
    • その機器には触ってはいけません。とても繊細です。

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences（長い例文）

  • You must remember your passport because we are flying early tomorrow morning, and there won’t be time to go back home.
    • 明日の朝早く飛行機に乗るので、取りに戻る時間がなく、パスポートを忘れてはいけません。
  • Students have to submit the report by Friday, but they should start writing it today to avoid last‑minute stress.
    • 生徒は金曜日までにレポートを提出しなければなりませんが、ギリギリのストレスを避けるために今日書き始めたほうがいいです。
  • It may rain this afternoon, so you should take an umbrella just in case, especially if you’ll be outside for a long time.
    • 午後に雨が降るかもしれないので、特に長時間外にいるなら、念のため傘を持っていったほうがいいです。
  • He might be late because the trains are delayed, and he said earlier that the line was very crowded.
    • 電車が遅れていて、路線が混んでいると言っていたので、彼は遅れるかもしれません。
  • We could visit the museum this weekend if we have time, but we might also stay home and relax.
    • 時間があれば今週末に博物館へ行くこともできますが、家でゆっくり過ごす可能性もあります。

🧩 4. Practice Exercises（練習問題）

Exercise A – Choose the correct option

1. You (must / may) wear a mask in the hospital.
must
2. She (should / must) drink more water.
should
3. He (may / must) be at home. I’m not sure.
may
4. They (have to / might) finish the project today because the deadline is tomorrow.
have to
5. It (could / must) be true, but I need to check.
could

Exercise B – Complete the sentence

1. You look exhausted. You ________ take a rest.
should
2. All staff ________ wear a badge in the building. It's the rule.
have to
3. I'm not sure where she is. She ________ be at the library.
may / might / could
4. I ________ finish this report today; my boss is waiting for it.
must
5. He hasn't decided yet. He ________ join us for dinner.
might / may

📖 5. Short Reading Text（短い読解文）

Preparing for a New Job

My new job starts next month, so I have to prepare many documents and complete several online trainings. I must check every detail carefully because even a small mistake could create serious problems later. I should also buy some new clothes for the office, since the dress code is stricter than at my previous job. While I was reading the company guidelines, I realized that I may need to update my computer as well.

The company may move to a bigger building next year, but nobody knows for sure. My manager said we might get more team members soon, which means the workload could increase. I could be working with new colleagues from different departments, so I should be ready for changes. I’m also thinking about taking a business communication course because it might help me adapt faster. It’s going to be a busy time, but I believe these changes could lead to valuable opportunities.

新しい仕事の準備

来月新しい仕事が始まるので、多くの書類を準備し、いくつかのオンライン研修も受けなければなりません。 小さな間違いでも後で大きな問題になる可能性があるため、すべてを注意深く確認しなければなりません。 前の職場より服装規定が厳しいため、オフィス用の新しい服も買ったほうがよさそうです。 会社のガイドラインを読んでいると、パソコンも更新する必要があるかもしれないと気づきました。

会社は来年もっと大きなビルに移転するかもしれませんが、まだ確かではありません。 マネージャーは、近いうちにチームメンバーが増えるかもしれないと言っていました。 そのため、仕事量が増える可能性があります。 別の部署の新しい人たちと働くことになるかもしれないので、変化に備えておくべきだと思います。 また、早く慣れるためにビジネスコミュニケーションの講座を受けようかとも考えています。 忙しくなりそうですが、これらの変化は良いチャンスにつながるかもしれません。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions（読解問題）

1. Why must the writer check the documents carefully?
Because even a small mistake could cause serious problems later.
2. Why does the writer need new clothes?
Because the office dress code is stricter than before.
3. What might happen next year at the company?
The company may move to a bigger building.
4. Why should the writer be ready for changes?
Because they could be working with new colleagues from different departments.
5. What course is the writer considering taking?
A business communication course.

🎯 7. Summary（まとめ）

  • Must = strong internal obligation.
    • 強い内的義務。
  • Have to = external rule or requirement.
    • 外部のルール・必要性。
  • Should = advice or expectation.
    • 助言・期待。
  • May / Might / Could = different degrees of possibility.
    • 可能性の度合い。
  • Context determines the correct modal verb.
    • 文脈が最重要。
