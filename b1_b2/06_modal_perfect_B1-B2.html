CEFR: B1-B2

Lesson 6: Modal Perfect

📚 1. Grammar Explanation（文法の説明）

1. Should have + past participle

Should have + past participle is used to express past advice, criticism, or regret about something that did not happen as expected. → 過去に「〜すべきだった」「〜しておけばよかった」という後悔・助言・非難を表す。

UseExample
Regret / missed actionI should have studied more before the exam; the questions were harder than I expected.
CriticismYou should have called me earlier so we could prepare together.
Advice in hindsightShe should have checked the schedule; the store was closed.

2. Could have + past participle

Could have + past participle expresses a past possibility or ability that existed, but the action did not happen. → 過去に「できたはず」「可能性があった」が、実際には起こらなかったこと。

UseExample
Missed opportunityWe could have won the game if we hadn’t made that mistake.
Past abilityShe could have helped you, but she didn’t know you needed support.
Unrealized potentialThey could have finished earlier with better planning.

3. Might have + past participle

Might have + past participle is used to talk about a past possibility when we are not certain. → 過去の出来事について「〜だったかもしれない」と推測するが、確信はない。

UseExample
Uncertain possibilityHe might have forgotten the meeting because he looked very tired this morning.
SpeculationThe noise might have come from the apartment upstairs.

✏️ 2. Short Example Sentences（短い例文）

  • I should have slept earlier; I was exhausted this morning.
    • もっと早く寝ればよかった。今朝とても疲れていました。
  • You should have told me before the meeting started.
    • 会議が始まる前に言ってくれればよかった。
  • She could have taken a taxi, but she chose to walk in the rain.
    • タクシーに乗れたはずですが、雨の中歩くことを選びました。
  • They could have finished earlier if they hadn’t taken such a long break.
    • 長い休憩を取らなければ、もっと早く終われたはずです。
  • He might have lost his keys on the train.
    • 電車で鍵をなくしたかもしれません。
  • It might have rained last night; the ground is still wet.
    • 昨夜雨が降ったかもしれません。地面がまだ濡れています。
  • I could have helped you if you had asked.
    • 頼んでくれれば手伝えたはずです。

📝 3. Longer Example Sentences（長い例文）

  • I should have checked the schedule before leaving, because the train was not running and I wasted a lot of time.
    • 電車が動いていなかったので、出発前に時刻表を確認すべきでした。時間を無駄にしました。
  • She could have joined us for dinner, but she decided to stay home and finish her assignment.
    • 彼女は夕食に参加できたはずですが、課題を終えるために家にいることにしました。
  • They might have misunderstood the message, which explains why they arrived so late.
    • メッセージを誤解したのかもしれず、そのせいで遅れて到着したのでしょう。
  • We should have brought more water because the hike was much longer and hotter than we expected.
    • 予想よりハイキングが長く暑かったので、もっと水を持ってくるべきでした。
  • He could have passed the test if he had studied harder, but he didn’t take it seriously.
    • もっと勉強していればテストに合格できたはずですが、真剣に取り組みませんでした。

3. They (might have / must have) forgotten the meeting.
might have
4. You (should have / could have) told me the truth.
should have
5. He (could have / might have) won the race, but he fell.
could have

Exercise B – Complete the sentence

1. I ________ have ________ (bring) an umbrella; I got completely soaked.
should / brought
2. She ________ have ________ (miss) the bus; that's why she was so late.
might / missed
3. They ________ have ________ (arrive) earlier if they hadn't stopped for lunch.
could / arrived

5. He ________ have ________ (take) the wrong train; he still hasn't arrived.

📖 5. Short Reading Text（短い読解文）

A Miscommunication at the Café

Last weekend, I planned to meet my friend at a café, but many things went wrong. First, I should have left home earlier because the trains were delayed due to an accident. When I finally arrived at the café, my friend was not there. She might have gone to another café nearby, or she could have misunderstood the meeting time. I tried calling her, but she didn’t answer. I waited for thirty minutes, thinking she might still come, but she never appeared.

Later, I checked my messages again and realized I could have read her text more carefully. She had written that she “might be a little late,” but I assumed she meant only a few minutes. She might have decided to go home after waiting somewhere else. In the end, we both should have confirmed the details more clearly. It could have been a nice afternoon, but our small mistakes caused a lot of confusion.

カフェでの行き違い

先週末、友達とカフェで会う予定でしたが、いろいろうまくいきませんでした。 まず、電車が事故で遅れていたので、もっと早く家を出るべきでした。 ようやくカフェに着いたとき、友達はいませんでした。 近くの別のカフェに行ったのかもしれないし、時間を誤解した可能性もあります。 電話をしてみましたが、出ませんでした。 30分待ちながら「来るかもしれない」と思っていましたが、結局来ませんでした。

その後、メッセージをもう一度確認して、もっと注意深く読むべきだったと気づきました。 彼女は「少し遅れるかもしれない」と書いていましたが、私は数分だけだと思い込んでいました。 彼女はどこかで待ったあと、帰ることにしたのかもしれません。 結局、私たちはもっとしっかり詳細を確認すべきでした。 本来なら楽しい午後になったはずですが、小さなミスが混乱を招いてしまいました。

❓ 6. Reading Comprehension Questions（読解問題）

1. Why should the writer have left home earlier?
Because the trains were delayed due to an accident.
2. What might the friend have done instead of going to the café?
She might have gone to another café nearby.
3. What could the writer have done more carefully?
They could have read the message more carefully.
4. Why might the friend have gone home?
Because she might have waited somewhere else and then given up.
5. What should both of them have done to avoid confusion?
They should have confirmed the meeting details more clearly.

🎯 7. Summary（まとめ）

  • Should have → past advice/regret.
    • 過去のアドバイス・後悔。
  • Could have → past possibility/ability.
    • 過去の可能性・能力。
  • Might have → uncertain past possibility.
    • 過去の不確かな可能性。
  • Useful for regrets and past hypotheses.
    • 後悔・仮説に便利。
